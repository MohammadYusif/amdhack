# Mihan — Competitive Landscape & Positioning

Why no one already occupies Mihan's square, and how to answer the incumbent
questions. Sourced from public 2025–2026 material (links at bottom).

## The market context (verified, citable)

- **SAMA formally licensed Open Banking on 26 March 2026** — moved from
  sandbox to a supervised licensing regime. The regulator's own framing
  names *"freelancers, gig workers, and individuals with multiple income
  streams"* as the beneficiaries. That is Mihan's thesis in SAMA's words.
- **Lean Technologies is the first licensed Open Banking provider** — and
  Mihan's data layer already routes through Lean. We build on licensed rails.
- **The +32% benchmark is Tamara using Lean's verified financial data** — a
  named, checkable case study (Lean Tech blog), not folklore. Always cite it
  as "Tamara + Lean," not just "Tamara."
- **Freelancer market: 1.8M+ active, 2.2M+ registered** on the national
  platform (2024) — up 40% on digital platforms 2020–2023.

## Who else is in the space — and why Mihan is a different square

| Player | What they do | Why it is NOT Mihan |
|---|---|---|
| **Tamara** | BNPL + cash-flow credit for gig workers; the +32% case | Proves the *method* works — but it's short-term BNPL, not a commercial-bank term loan. Mihan brings the method into a bank. |
| **Lendo** | SME *invoice* financing; SAMA-regulated; building ML underwriting | B2B, invoice-collateralized. Not consumer freelancer cash-flow underwriting. |
| **Social Development Bank** | Subsidized freelancer financing up to SAR 200K | Development-bank/subsidized, mandate-driven — not commercial creditworthiness underwriting priced on risk. |
| **Alinma Freelance Card** (Apr 2025, with SDB) | Interest-free, SDB-funded, ≤ SAR 48K, 120+ professions | A subsidized on-ramp, not a credit relationship. See the graduation narrative below. |

**Mihan's defensible square:** the first *commercial-bank, cash-flow-underwritten,
risk-priced term loan for freelancers, embedded in the bank's own app, on
SAMA-licensed Open Banking rails.* Tamara proved the method; Lendo does SMEs;
SDB subsidizes. None of them is standing here.

## ⭐ The graduation narrative (our answer to "Alinma already has a freelance product")

Alinma launched the Kingdom's first **Freelance Card** in April 2025 with the
Social Development Bank: interest-free, SDB-funded, capped at SAR 48,000. It is
a **subsidized on-ramp** — it does not assess the freelancer's creditworthiness
and it lends the development bank's money, not Alinma's.

> **"Alinma already proved it believes in this segment with the Freelance Card.
> But that product is SDB-funded and capped — a subsidized on-ramp, not a
> credit relationship. Mihan is the graduation path: it turns a freelancer with
> a proven cash-flow history into a commercially underwritten borrower Alinma
> lends its own capital to, at scale and priced to risk. We're not proposing a
> new bet — we're completing one Alinma already made."**

This converts the strongest incumbent objection into proof of strategic fit.
If we advance to the next stage, this becomes its own slide (before the
business case) — the deck already submitted does not include it.

## Q&A the landscape will trigger

- **"You already have a Freelance Card — why Mihan?"** → the graduation
  narrative above. Subsidized/capped/SDB-funded vs. commercial/scalable/
  risk-priced. Different product, same strategic direction.
- **"Isn't this just Tamara?"** → Tamara is BNPL and proved the method (+32%).
  Mihan is a bank term loan (SAR 25–60K) with SAMA-grade explainability, DBR
  compliance, and a human officer. Different product, same underlying insight.
- **"Lendo does credit scoring too."** → Lendo underwrites SMEs against
  invoices. Mihan underwrites individuals against 18 months of verified
  cross-bank cash flow. Different borrower, different data.
- **"Why does Alinma need this vs. leaving it to fintechs?"** → Open Banking
  lets the bank keep the primary relationship, deposits, and cross-sell instead
  of ceding the segment to BNPL players. First-mover data moat.
